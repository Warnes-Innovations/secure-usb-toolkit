🔐 Secure USB Drive

This USB drive contains a password-protected encrypted area for securely storing files. The encrypted storage is powered by VeraCrypt, a free and open-source encryption tool trusted by security professionals worldwide.

Your files are stored inside SECURE_DATA.vc on the second partition of this drive. To access them, you need VeraCrypt installed on your computer.

⚠️ Important: If you forget your password, your data cannot be recovered — not even by the person who gave you this drive. Keep your password safe.

1 Install VeraCrypt

A copy of the VeraCrypt installer for Windows is included on this USB drive.

🪟 Windows — bundled installer

  1. Open the VeraCrypt folder on this USB drive (the same partition as this file).
  2. Run VeraCrypt Setup 1.26.24.exe and follow the on-screen instructions. Click through the defaults — no special configuration needed.

Prefer to download directly? The latest version is always available at
https://veracrypt.io/en/Downloads.html

🍎 macOS — two installs required

VeraCrypt on macOS requires a FUSE driver to mount encrypted volumes. You must install FUSE-T first, then VeraCrypt. FUSE-T is kext-free and works on both Intel and Apple Silicon Macs.

  1. Install FUSE-T.
    A copy of the FUSE-T installer is included on this USB drive in the VeraCrypt folder (fuse-t-macos-installer-*.pkg). Double-click it and follow the prompts. You may be asked for your password and to approve a system extension in System Settings → Privacy & Security.
  2. Install VeraCrypt (FUSE-T build).
    Open the VeraCrypt folder on this USB drive and run VeraCrypt_FUSE-T_*.dmg. Drag the app to your Applications folder.

Prefer to download directly? Get both from the official sites:
FUSE-T: https://www.fuse-t.org/
VeraCrypt: https://veracrypt.io/en/Downloads.html (choose the FUSE-T compatible version)

2 Open Your Encrypted Files

🪟 Windows

  1. Open the VeraCrypt application from your Start menu.
  2. Click Select File… in the middle of the window.
  3. Browse to the second partition of this USB drive and open SECURE_DATA.vc.
    (The second partition may appear as a separate drive letter in Windows Explorer.)
  4. In the list at the top of VeraCrypt, click on an unused drive letter (e.g. Z:).
  5. Click Mount.
  6. Enter your password and click OK.
  7. Your files now appear in Windows Explorer under the drive letter you selected. Use them just like any other folder.

🍎 macOS

  1. Open the VeraCrypt application.
  2. Click Select File… and navigate to the USB drive's second partition.
  3. Select SECURE_DATA.vc and click Open.
  4. Click Mount, enter your password, then click OK.
  5. Your files appear in Finder → Locations as a new mounted volume.

🐧 Linux

  1. Open VeraCrypt (or run veracrypt in a terminal).
  2. Click Select File… and choose SECURE_DATA.vc.
  3. Select a free slot number, click Mount, and enter your password.
  4. Your files are accessible at the path shown in the VeraCrypt window.

3 Close Safely When Done

It is important to properly dismount the encrypted drive before unplugging the USB. Unplugging without dismounting can corrupt your files.

  1. Close any files or applications that are using files from the encrypted drive.
  2. In VeraCrypt, select the mounted volume and click Dismount.
  3. Eject or safely remove the USB drive from your operating system before unplugging it.
